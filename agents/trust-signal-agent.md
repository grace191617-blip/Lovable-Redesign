## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Authority Markers
- [ ] CPA credential visible on every page (navbar or hero)
- [ ] Geographic specificity (Menlo Park, Silicon Valley) establishes local authority
- [ ] Proprietary frameworks (Tax Operating System, Strategic Tax Architecture™) signal expertise
- [ ] Professional photography or headshot on About page (not stock)
- [ ] Industry-specific language demonstrates domain expertise

### 2. Social Proof Distribution
- [ ] At least one testimonial per major service page
- [ ] Testimonials include full name, location, and title/role
- [ ] Testimonials address specific outcomes, not generic praise
- [ ] Testimonial placement near CTAs (proximity to conversion point)
- [ ] Consider adding logos of client companies (with permission)

### 3. Transparency & Honesty
- [ ] Pricing published openly — no "call for quote" on standard services
- [ ] "Custom" pricing clearly explained (what triggers custom vs. standard)
- [ ] Engagement process described step-by-step (what to expect)
- [ ] "What This Is Not" sections demonstrate honesty and self-selection
- [ ] Qualification criteria help prospects self-assess fit

### 4. Risk Reduction
- [ ] "Most clients start with a conversation" lowers commitment barrier
- [ ] No long-term contracts implied for initial engagements
- [ ] Consultation framed as "determine fit" not "sales call"
- [ ] Clear deliverables listed (strategy memorandum, calendar, projections)
- [ ] Timeline expectations set (e.g., "4–6 weeks for Intensive")

### 5. Professional Presentation
- [ ] Consistent brand voice: confident, clear, not salesy
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] No broken links or placeholder content
- [ ] Footer includes relevant legal/professional disclosures
- [ ] Contact information easily accessible

### 6. Digital Trust Signals
- [ ] SSL certificate active (HTTPS)
- [ ] Professional domain name
- [ ] Fast page load times (no trust-eroding delays)
- [ ] Mobile experience is polished (not just functional)
- [ ] No intrusive popups or aggressive lead capture

### 7. Competitive Differentiation
- [ ] Clear articulation of what makes this firm different
- [ ] "Systems thinking" positioning distinct from generic CPA messaging
- [ ] Founder/operator focus creates niche authority
- [ ] Advisory vs. compliance distinction clearly communicated
- [ ] Silicon Valley context leveraged authentically

## Trust Gap Analysis

### Strong Trust Signals Present
- Proprietary framework branding (Tax Operating System)
- Transparent pricing with detailed tier comparisons
- Qualification criteria that self-select ideal clients
- Professional, restrained design aesthetic

### Trust Signals to Strengthen
- Additional testimonials across service pages
- Case studies or anonymized outcome examples
- Professional headshot and team bios
- Industry association memberships or certifications
- Media mentions or speaking engagements
- Client count or years of experience statistics

## Review Cadence
Run after any content update, new testimonial addition, or page launch. Cross-reference with CRO Agent for conversion impact of trust signals and SEO Agent for schema markup of reviews and credentials.
